This course is for the person who lives closest to the quality of data in CluedIn. The goal is not to turn a steward into a platform administrator. The goal is to build a confident operator who can move through the instance, recognize what good and bad data looks like, trace where issues came from, choose the correct remediation path, and work productively with Data Architects.

Unlike a lightweight tour, this course is written to stand on its own. The learner should be able to read each module and understand the workflow, the screen, the decisions, the language, and the expected outcome without having to open other documentation pages just to fill basic knowledge gaps. Existing reference articles are still useful, but they are not required for learning the flow.

## What this course teaches

By the end of this course, a learner should be able to:

- explain how source records become golden records
- move confidently through Ingestion, Search, record pages, Clean, Deduplication, Glossary, Governance, and AI-assisted remediation surfaces
- use search, filters, saved searches, and record inspection to isolate operational data issues
- understand when to use validations, clean projects, deduplication, tags, glossary terms, or AI jobs
- document findings well enough for a Data Architect to improve mappings, identifiers, rules, or streams
- understand the downstream impact of steward actions on consumers and export targets

## Recommended audience

This course fits Data Stewards, data quality analysts, master data operators, and business users who own ongoing data-quality work but are not the primary owners of platform design decisions.

## How to use this course

Read the modules in order. The narrative intentionally follows the same order that work usually becomes visible in CluedIn:

1. set up your steward operating model
1. understand what a golden record is
1. understand how ingestion and mapping decisions influence stewardship
1. find and inspect the records that need attention
1. check source quality before records become bigger problems
1. fix recurring issues with clean projects
1. review and merge duplicates safely
1. organize quality work with glossary terms and tags
1. use AI in a controlled way
1. understand what your changes do downstream
1. prove that you can run the full loop repeatedly

## Suggested pacing

A strong learning rhythm is to complete one module per day and perform the practice exercise before moving on. The course is written so that it can also be delivered as a structured two-week onboarding track.

## What this course is not

This course is not a full Data Architect course, not a deep integration build guide, and not a complete reference for every CluedIn module. It teaches the steward how to operate well, when to escalate, and how to work in a way that improves trust in the platform.
